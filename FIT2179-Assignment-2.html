First Vega-Lite graph

This graph describes the rate of unemployment throughout the past 35 years.

It has gradually declined since the 1990s before spiking during the Great Recession and again during the COVID-19 pandemic.

After falling during the early 2020s, the unemployment rate is slowly creeping back up and is likely to spike again due to tech related layoffs.

This graph describes the percentage of women who are unemployed throughout the past 35 years.

The rate of female unemployment has actually stayed similar to the overall unemployment rate.
